**Barbell Squat:**

- Primary Muscle Group: Quadriceps (thighs)
- Secondary Muscle Groups: Glutes (buttocks), hamstrings (back of thighs), and core muscles. 

**Benefits:**

- Builds lower body strength and muscle mass
- Improves balance and stability
- Enhances athletic performance
- Increases power output

**Proper Form:**

1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly turned out.
2. Grasp the barbell with an overhand grip, hands slightly wider than your shoulders.
3. Bend your knees and hips and lower your body until your thighs are parallel to the ground. 
4. Keep your back straight and your core engaged throughout the movement.
5. Press through your heels and return to the starting position.

**Variations:**

- **Front Squat:** Hold the barbell across the front of your shoulders, resting on your clavicles.
- **Overhead Squat:** Hold the barbell overhead with your arms locked out.
- **Goblet Squat:** Hold a dumbbell or kettlebell in front of your chest.

**Safety Tips:**

- Start with a light weight and gradually increase it as you get stronger.
- Maintain a stable and controlled movement throughout the exercise.
- Keep your knees in line with your toes and avoid allowing them to cave inward.
